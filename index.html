About

I am a postdoctoral fellow at Ariel University, working with Prof. Sergey Bondarenko. My work focuses on semiclassical and Euclidean approaches to gravity, with the goal of making Euclidean continuation, boundary terms, and observer-dependent constructions precise and covariant.

I am especially interested in extracting operational outputs—Euclidean action, temperature/entropy, and phase/holonomy data—that can be compared across semiclassical gravity, QFT in curved spacetime, and controlled toy models.

Research areas

Euclidean Quantum Gravity

Euclidean methods in semiclassical gravity with emphasis on covariant Euclideanization, boundary terms, and thermodynamic interpretations of horizons.

  • Covariant Euclidean continuation & complex saddles
  • GHY/corner terms and action-based thermodynamics
  • Observer-covariant formulations of regularity/temperature

Quantum Gravity Phenomenology

Operational and effective probes of quantum-gravitational effects, tied to curvature data and controlled reduced dynamics.

  • Curvature-induced decoherence / universal bounds
  • Effective dynamics from semiclassical consistency
  • Relativistic/covariant uncertainty structures (GUP-like)

Theoretical Cosmology

Semiclassical and Euclidean approaches to early-universe questions, including initial conditions and Euclidean–Lorentzian matching.

  • Euclidean caps / contour proposals for cosmological states
  • Geometric mechanisms in early-universe dynamics
  • Links between action principles and cosmological observables
