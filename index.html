About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at Ariel University, Israel, working with Prof. Sergey Bondarenko. My research focuses on the interface between gravity and quantum theory — particularly in understanding how semiclassical and quantum features of spacetime manifest at different physical regimes.

At heart I am driven by foundational questions. What does it mean for spacetime to acquire microstructure at the Planck scale? Can semiclassical consistency already hint at the rules of full quantum gravity? These puzzles guide my day‑to‑day calculations — whether I am tracking how extrinsic curvature corrects horizon entropy via a covariant Wick rotation, or showing that tidal components of the Riemann tensor set universal bounds on gravitational decoherence.

Beyond the equations I value open collaboration, clear communication, and the creative playfulness that theoretical physics affords. Whether in seminars, conferences, or impromptu whiteboard sessions, I see research as a collective endeavour: refining our questions is often as illuminating as chasing the answers.

Research

Quantum & Semiclassical Gravity

Planck‑scale quantum effects and their imprint on classically curved space‑time.

Black‑Hole Physics

Interior structure, horizon thermodynamics and information retention.

Quantum Geometry

Covariant uncertainty principles and Wick‑rotated metric phases.

Early‑Universe Cosmology

Geometric mechanisms capable of seeding, driving and ending cosmic inflation.

Core Research Questions & Long‑Term Goals

  • Can space‑time singularities be removed within a covariant semiclassical framework?
  • What are the truly relativistic limits of quantum uncertainty?
  • How does Wick rotation encode thermodynamics and quantum phases of gravity?
  • Does curvature itself decohere quantum matter?

Vision ⟶ A covariant, experimentally anchored picture of quantum space‑time where horizons, curvature and quantum information are treated on equal footing — linking black‑hole interiors, Planck‑scale physics and the dynamics of the early universe.

Current Work & Selected Outputs

Relativistic GUP

First coordinate‑free formulation of the Generalised Uncertainty Principle, tightening phenomenological bounds.

Phys. Rev. D 105 (2022)

Covariant Wick Rotation

Shows how Euclidean “caps” on Lorentzian manifolds encode horizon entropy and path‑integral phases.

EPJ C 83 (2023)

Quantum Decoherence in Curved Space‑Time

Derives curvature‑dependent decoherence times, suggesting satellite‑based experimental tests.

arXiv 2302.09038

Black‑Hole Interior Modelling

Two‑phase quasiparticle core that avoids the central singularity while respecting horizon thermodynamics.

arXiv 2506.02944

In Progress

Singularity‑Avoiding Black‑Hole Interiors ▸

Extending the quasiparticle‑core model to include quantum back‑reaction from Hawking partners, aiming for a horizon‑to‑center description embedded in effective field theory.

Geometric Models of Inflation ▸

Using covariant Wick‑rotated metrics to construct “Euclidean bridge” geometries that interpolate between pre‑inflationary and FRW epochs, offering naturally graceful exit conditions.
